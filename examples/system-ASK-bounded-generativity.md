# system-ASK as a bounded-generativity instance

## Role in the method

`system-ASK` — the ASK practice taken as a whole, apex and ecology together — is itself a concrete instance of the bounded-generativity topology defined in `docs/bounded-generativity.md`. The primitive's worked instances (§Worked instances) run from `isometric-cubes-ASK` through `mazeASK`, `asset-pipeline-ASK`, studioLeoV, generative-AI workflows, and the ASK advisory / extended-cognition loop. This example takes the last of those one altitude up: the same structure that governs a single advisory loop also governs the standing architecture of the whole practice.

This is a worked-example explanation at method altitude — not a second primitive definition and not an operational authorization. The primitive is defined in `docs/bounded-generativity.md`; this maps how `system-ASK` realizes it.

## The practice-level mapping

The bounded-generativity roles map onto `system-ASK` as follows:

- **source of intent / normative apex** — `human-ASK`: supplies purpose, the governing standard, authority, closure, and recourse.
- **grammar and aperture** — the question, the source-of-intent frame, the governing standard, constraints, open decisions, and the delegated scope that frame a given engagement.
- **bounded realization field** — `ecology-ASK`: advisor surfaces, execution surfaces, repositories, critics, evidence, and domain contributors, producing candidate distinctions and artifacts.
- **authorized judgment where present** — scoped expert, executor, or delegated decisions made inside the granted scope.
- **selection + closure** — ASK accepts, rejects, revises, holds, or relays what becomes operative.
- **artifact governance** — review, merge, canonicalization, absorption, provenance, and closure.
- **governed output** — the merged repo artifact, canonical note, adopted decision, or governed record.

## The core relation

`system-ASK` realizes the bounded-generativity topology at the practice level: `human-ASK` supplies the source of intent and closure; `ecology-ASK` supplies the bounded realization field; selected outcomes become durable through governance. In the primitive's relation-chain form:

`order from chaos >> bounded generativity >> selection + closure >> artifact governance >> governed artifact + governance record`

The recurrence is the point. The same topology holds at three scopes without changing shape: within a single generative artifact (`isometric-cubes-ASK`, `mazeASK`, image-making), within a single cognition loop (advisory / extended-cognition), and across the whole practice (this example). Second-and-beyond instances are what disambiguate project-specific shape from the cross-project type; the practice-level instance is the broadest of them.

## Boundary

This is a descriptive structural classification of `system-ASK`'s topology. It does not claim that every `system-ASK` operation is correctly bounded, that every judgment is right, or that the system proves its own legitimacy — only that the roles, boundaries, and direction of authority have the bounded-generativity shape. The structure secures answerability in principle; whether it is discharged well in any given case is a separate, non-self-certifying question.

The direction of authority is invariant: intent originates at the apex and is not synthesized upward from the realization field. `human-ASK` sits inside the whole `system-ASK` boundary while remaining outside the bounded-generativity subframe it governs — the apex governing the field, not a paradox.

Ownership follows the layer model:

- **`method-ASK`** owns the primitive and this cross-surface structural relation.
- **`control-surface`** owns the execution protocols that move work across the relation — routing, handoffs, critique, review, instantiation, relay, and closure.
- **project repos** own their domain instances.
