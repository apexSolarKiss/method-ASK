# image-making // source of intent

## Role in the method

Image-making is the worked domain that proves the [normative apex](../docs/normative-apex.md), the judgment topology, and [artifact governance](../docs/governance.md) hold across **tool stacks** — photography, transformation, and generation. The role is structural, not art-historical: the domain is the worked example; the primitives are the durable element. The full evidence base, mechanisms, and anchors live operator-side; this surface carries only what makes the method principles visible, once.

The thesis the domain proves: **authorship lives in source of intent, bounded discretion, judgment-bearing intervention, selection, and closure — not in the apparatus, the provenance, or the pixels.** A new tool stack changes who or what executes; it does not relocate the apex.

## What this surface proves

- **Capture is selection (Szarkowski).** Lens-based photography selects from an actuality that exceeds the image: the frame, the vantage point, the parcel of time, and the detail are authorial cuts, not neutral transcription. The apparatus performs causal work; the source of intent decides which relation becomes durable. Authorship is present even when the hand leaves no visible trace — the clean public grammar of *selection · frame · time · vantage point.*

- **Delegated realization (Sol LeWitt).** A wall drawing separates LeWitt's source of intent and plan from the drafter who realizes it. The certificate carries and authenticates authorized lineage; it does not originate intent. The work survives non-identical realizations and even retained errors. **Delegation is not transfer of source of intent.** It is also the cleanest case of the judgment topology: judgment is classified by the **execution span**, not by who executes — a drafter interpreting a score executes *and* judges; a model sampling candidates executes *without* judging. A span is judgment-bearing when an authorized decision-maker — human or computational — evaluates admissible alternatives against the governing standard and binds within scope.

- **Aggregation is not an apex (Komar & Melamid, *America's Most Wanted*).** A statistical composite of polled preferences can constrain a painting without becoming its source of intent: the respondents judged, the arithmetic did not. **Judgment in the inputs does not propagate through synthesis.** The work is deeply authored by the artists who framed the procedure and closed the result; the average is material, not author. The constituting move — a poll aggregates, a constituted collective decides — is the image-domain instance of [constitutive governance](../docs/governance.md).
